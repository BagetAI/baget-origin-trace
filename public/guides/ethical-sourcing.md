# Direct Trade vs Fair Trade: The Truth About Coffee Sourcing

**TL;DR**: While "Fair Trade" provides a safety net, "Direct Trade" (and specifically Roasted-at-Origin) builds a ladder. Learn why Origin Trace prioritizes radical transparency and direct value retention to ensure more profit stays with the farmers who grow your beans.

## The Certification Paradox
For decades, the "Fair Trade" logo has been the primary signal for ethical coffee consumption. It established a vital floor—a minimum price to protect farmers against market crashes. However, as the specialty coffee industry has evolved, the "floor" has become a ceiling. The certification process is often expensive for smallholders and focuses on commodity-grade volume rather than quality-driven equity.

## What is Direct Trade?
Direct Trade is not a certification; it's a relationship. It involves roasters visiting farms, cupping beans on-site, and negotiating prices directly with the producers. At Origin Trace, we take this a step further. We don't just "visit"; we partner with roasters who are physically located in the producing countries (Thailand, Colombia, Ethiopia). 

### Why Direct Trade Wins for Connoisseurs:
1. **Price Transparency**: In a typical supply chain, a farmer might receive 5-10% of the retail price. In our Roasted-at-Origin model, that number can jump to 30-50%.
2. **Quality Incentives**: When farmers are paid for quality (SCA scores) rather than volume, they can invest in better processing, shade-grown techniques, and sustainable labor.
3. **The Traceability Feed**: We provide the "FOB" (Freight on Board) price for every batch. This is the amount paid to the producer before shipping and taxes. If you can't see the FOB, it isn't truly transparent.

## The "Roasted-at-Origin" Difference
Most "Direct Trade" roasters in the US or Europe buy green beans and ship them thousands of miles to be roasted in a warehouse. This "de-values" the origin. By roasting at the source—in places like Chiang Mai with Akha Ama or Bogotá with Azahar—the high-value manufacturing step (roasting) stays in the local economy.

## Impact Beyond the Cup
Every ethical coffee subscription should be able to answer one question: "How much did the picker make?" We focus on projects like the Azahar "Pickers Project," which specifically targets the seasonal labor force that is often left out of even the most "ethical" certification schemes.

## Conclusion: Brew with Purpose
Choosing a small batch coffee subscription shouldn't just be about flavor—it's about the trace. When you brew a cup from Origin Trace, you aren't just drinking 84+ point specialty coffee; you're participating in a rebalanced global economy.
