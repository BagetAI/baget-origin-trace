# The Connoisseur’s Map: How to Navigate Specialty Coffee Microlots

**TL;DR**: High-end coffee is a world of data. From SCA scores to processing methods, learn how to decode the labels and find the perfect microlot for your brewing style.

## What is a Microlot?
In the coffee industry, a "lot" is a specific batch of beans. A "microlot" is a hyper-specific selection—often from a single hillside of a single farm, or a single day's harvest. These represent the absolute peak of a farmer's production. They are rare, limited, and possess unique flavor profiles that are lost when blended.

## Decoding the SCA Score
The Specialty Coffee Association (SCA) uses a 100-point scale to grade coffee. 
* **80-84**: Very Good (Standard specialty)
* **85-89**: Excellent (Our baseline at Origin Trace)
* **90+**: Outstanding (The "Holy Grail" microlots)
When you join our small batch coffee subscription, you are consistently accessing 84+ and 90+ point coffees that are rarely found in local retail.

## Understanding the Process
The way a coffee is "processed" (how the fruit is removed from the seed) defines its flavor as much as the origin.
1. **Washed (Wet Process)**: The fruit is removed before drying. Result: Clean, bright acidity, tea-like body.
2. **Natural (Dry Process)**: The bean dries inside the fruit. Result: Heavy body, intense fruitiness, wine-like notes.
3. **Honey Process**: Some fruit (mucilage) is left on during drying. Result: Sweetness, balanced acidity, creamy texture.

## Why Independent Roasters?
Small, independent roasters are the curators of the coffee world. Unlike massive chains that prioritize consistency (which often means roasting away the unique traits), independent roasters roast to highlight the specific terroir of the microlot. 

## The Connoisseur’s Ritual
To truly appreciate a microlot, try "cupping" it at home or using a pour-over method like the Hario V60. These methods allow you to "trace" the flavor notes from the first sip to the lingering finish.
