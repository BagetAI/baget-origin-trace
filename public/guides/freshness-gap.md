# The Science of Freshness: Why Roasted-at-Origin Changes Everything

**TL;DR**: The "Freshness Gap" is the silent killer of coffee flavor. Discover why traditional shipping models fail your palate and how our 72-hour roast-to-door delivery preserves the volatile aromatics that define specialty coffee.

## The 15-Minute Rule
In the world of the coffee connoisseur, there are three stages of freshness: 15 months (green beans), 15 days (roasted beans), and 15 minutes (ground beans). Most "premium" coffees sold in grocery stores or even high-end cafes are already past their peak by the time they hit your grinder.

## The "Freshness Gap" Explained
Standard coffee logistics look like this: Green beans are harvested, stored in a warehouse, shipped across an ocean (taking 4-8 weeks), stored in another warehouse, roasted, packaged, and then shipped to you. Even the best small batch coffee loses its soul in this timeline. 

### What Happens to Old Coffee?
1. **Oxidation**: Once roasted, coffee beans are highly porous. Oxygen enters the bean, reacting with oils to create "stale" flavors (paper, cardboard, bitterness).
2. **Degassing**: Roasted beans release Carbon Dioxide. While some degassing is necessary (first 24-48 hours), the loss of CO2 is also the loss of the volatile compounds that create floral, fruity, and nutty notes.
3. **Oil Rancidity**: High-quality lipids in the bean begin to break down, turning bright acidity into flat, muddy flavors.

## The Origin Trace Solution: Decentralized Fulfillment
We solve the freshness gap by eliminating the middle warehouse. 
* **Day 1**: Your coffee is roasted by an independent artisan at the source (e.g., Onyx in Arkansas or Akha Ama in Thailand).
* **Day 1.5**: The beans are sealed in one-way valve bags, allowing degassing while preventing oxidation.
* **Day 2-3**: Express international couriers move the beans directly to your door.

## Why 72 Hours Matters
For most specialty coffee, the "Peak Flavor Window" opens at Day 3 and starts to close by Day 14. By the time our box arrives, you are at the exact moment the beans have stabilized and are ready to release their full complexity.

## How to Preserve Your Small Batch Coffee
Once your Origin Trace delivery arrives, follow these rules:
* **Never Refrigerate**: Moisture is the enemy.
* **Airtight is Essential**: Use our resealable bags or a vacuum canister.
* **Grind on Demand**: Never buy pre-ground if you care about the trace.
